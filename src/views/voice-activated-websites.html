Modern voice
activated websites

by Obscurial Consulting on 01/05/2018

With the recent popularity boom of personal assistant devices a question popped into our mind, "Why not use speech to control a website?"

At first, the idea of a voice feature raised some skeptical doubts, but after we evaluated the potential benefits, we came to the conclusion that it might actually be pretty useful(and fun).

See implementation here

Short short background

Voice has become an important factor to consider when designing and developing software. The trend is to develop more for the invisible and shift away from screens.

Speech executed commands in apps and on physical devices are getting exponentially more sophisticated. Why can't we bring the same level of complexity to the web?

Personal assistant devices span a multitude of apps and services in an incredibly fast-expanding ecosystem. Nowadays, Amazon's Alexa can even sing for you. Furthermore, with the increase of internet speed and easier media delivery, consumers are starting to replace music with podcasts, leaving no doubt, that it is only the beginning for voice interfaces.

The trend is beginning to catch up in Denmark as more web platforms are taking advantage of speech enhanced software. In Copenhagen, we foresee that the sectors which will in the near future invest the most in voice services are Government offices, Telco, and Healthcare. But don't take our words for granted.

Advantages:

What determined us to refer to a speech to text feature:

  • Less friction
    Users don't have to type or click anything.
  • Persons with disabilities
    It is a great way to improve the accessibility of your website.
  • New ways of structuring information
    To accomodate a voice interface the website structure has to be thought in a different manner.
  • More fun
    The user journey instantly becomes more captivating and interactive.

Our experiment

We decided to conduct a little experiment, by adding a feature which would allow users to navigate by using a set of defined voice commands. The feature is started manually, and once that happens, the user can say the name of a page and navigation occurs.

A small improvement to the overall browsing experience, but the same technique can achieve more complex tasks:

  • Voice biometrics
  • Instruct to perform background tasks
  • Voice search
  • Ask for the opening hours of a business
  • Change the layout of a page
  • Hands free interaction in limited reach working environments(ex: mechanics)
  • Text to speech, etc.

Providing feedback to the users

We are trying to communicate with a machine the way we communicate with humans and as we all know, people communicate by having dialogues. In that manner, we have to make sure that our system can provide feedback, thus having a conversation instead of a sad monologue.

Behold, the "vobble".

When the platform recognizes audio input, the bubble will turn green.

If the audio is recognized as speech, it will try to figure out what we mean.
On match, the application navigates, and the "vobble" resets to its initial state.

When the word provided does not match our list of pages,
the vobble blinks red for 2 seconds and reverts to its initial state.

Technologies used

Research has led us to all sorts of speech recognition frameworks and services. They come in all colors and flavors, some providing the basic voice recognition software, while more advanced ones offer deep learning and context-based algorithms.

Services we considered using:

IBM Watson Speech to text
https://www.ibm.com/watson/services/speech-to-text
Too many features for our small experiment

Google Cloud Speech to text
https://cloud.google.com/speech-to-text
No free developer possibility. Only Trial based subscription.

In the end, we decided upon using the native Chrome Web Speech API for the fact that it is convenient, easy to use, and provides the voice recognition feature we were looking for.

Conclusion

In conclusion, a voice-controlled/activated website can bring benefits to the overall browsing experience, as long as it is built as an extra layer on top of your application and does not impair a user that wants to use the peripheral devices.
